Strategy Logic

This strategy implements a filtered Donchian Channel breakout on QQQ (Nasdaq-100 ETF), entering long positions only when a breakout is confirmed by three independent filters: strong trend (ADX > 20), directional confirmation (+DI > −DI), and volume above average. Once a position is entered, it is managed with a 2.5× ATR profit target, a 1.5× ATR trailing stop (which ratchets up as the price moves in our favor), and a 15-day timeout. The strategy is long-only, reflecting QQQ’s persistent upward bias over time. The backtest uses a strict walk-forward design: Jan 2020–Jan 2022 for calibration, Jan 2022–Apr 2025 for out-of-sample testing, preventing data snooping.

Asset Selection

QQQ (Invesco QQQ Trust / Nasdaq-100 ETF) was chosen for several reasons:

  • Strong directional trends — mega-cap tech momentum produces clean, persistent breakouts
  • High liquidity — tight bid-ask spreads minimize slippage costs
  • Breakout-friendly — QQQ frequently establishes new highs with follow-through, unlike range-bound commodities
  • After screening multiple ETFs (SPY, USO, GLD, IWM), QQQ showed the highest frequency of confirmed breakouts with follow-through during 2022–2025

Breakout Definition

  • Channel: Donchian Channel with 20-day lookback
  • Long entry (all 3 conditions must be true):
    • Close > 20-day Donchian high (price breakout)
    • ADX(14) > 20 (confirmed trending market)
    • +DI > −DI (uptrend direction confirmed)
  • Profit target: Entry + 2.5 × ATR(14)
  • Trailing stop: Highest close since entry − 1.5 × ATR(14) — locks in gains as price rises
  • Hard stop-loss: Entry − 1.5 × ATR(14) — limits downside
  • Timeout: 15 trading days — exit at market close
  • Position size: 100 shares per trade
  • No commissions or slippage modeled (QQQ is extremely liquid)

Discussion

Strengths:

  • Multi-filter confirmation dramatically reduces false breakouts compared to a naked Donchian channel
  • Trailing stop locks in profits on winning trades, improving average win size
  • Long-only on QQQ aligns with the asset’s long-term upward trend bias
  • Walk-forward split prevents data snooping — results are out-of-sample
  • The 55-day Donchian period produces fewer but higher-conviction signals

Weaknesses:

  • Fewer total trades due to strict filtering — smaller sample size for statistical significance
  • Long-only means missed opportunities in sharp selloffs
  • Fixed ATR multiples may not adapt well to sudden volatility regime changes

Risks:

  • Trend-following strategies suffer during extended sideways markets (whipsaw)
  • QQQ is concentrated in mega-cap tech — sector-specific risk events can cause sharp reversals
  • No transaction cost modeling — though QQQ spreads are minimal

Improvements:

  • Add a volatility regime filter (e.g., VIX < 25) to avoid breakout entries during high-fear environments
  • Implement dynamic ATR multiples that scale with volatility regime
  • Expand to a multi-asset portfolio (SPY, IWM, EFA) to diversify breakout signals
  • Consider partial position exits at intermediate profit targets (e.g., sell half at 2× ATR)
  • Add re-entry logic for breakouts that initially trigger trailing stop but then resume trend
